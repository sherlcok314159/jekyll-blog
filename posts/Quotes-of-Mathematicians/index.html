Quotes of Mathematicians

Posted Jul 23, 2022 Updated Jul 23, 2022
3 min read
  1. Life is complex, and it has both real and imaginary parts. — Someone
  2. Basically, I’m not interested in doing research and I never have been… I’m interested in understanding, which is quite a different thing. And often to understand something you have to work it out yourself because no one else has done it. — David Blackwell
  3. To not know maths is a severe limitation to understanding the world. — Richard Feynman
  4. Problems worthy of attack prove their worth by fighting back. — Piet Hein
  5. An expert is a person who has made all the mistakes that can be made in a very narrow field. — Niels Bohr
  6. The beauty of mathematics is only shown to its patient followers. — Maryam Mirzakhani
  7. The essence of Mathematics lies precisely in its freedom. — Georg Cantor
  8. We must know, we will know. — David Hilbert
  9. If your tendency is to make sense out of chaos, start chaos. — Carlos Castaneda
  10. Perhaps I could best describe my experience of doing mathematics in terms of entering a dark mansion. You go into the first room and it’s dark, completely dark. You stumble around, bumping into the furniture. Gradually, you learn where each piece of furniture. And finally, after six months or so, you find the light switch and turn it on. Suddenly, it’s all illuminated and you can see exactly where you were. Then you enter the next dark room… — Andrew Wiles
  11. If we knew what it is we were doing, it would not be called research. Would it? — Albert Einstein
  12. If you can’t solve a problem, then there is an easier problem you can solve: find it. — George Pólya
  13. Young man, in mathematics you don’t understand things. You just get used to them. — John Von Neumann
  14. Mathematics is the art of giving the same name to different things. — Henri Poincare
  15. Mathematics is the subject in which we never know what we’re talking about, nor whether what we are saying is true. — Bertrand Russell
  16. All mathematics models are wrong. Some of them are useful. — George Box
  17. Whenever something goes wrong, remember 1+1 is still 2. — Someone
  18. The study of mathematics is apt to commence in disappointment… We are told that by its aid the stars are weighed and the billions of molecules in a drop of water are counted. Yet, like the ghost of Hamlet’s father, this great science eludes the efforts of our mental weapons to grasp it. — A. N. Whitehead
  19. When one does a theoretical calculation, there are two ways of doing it. Either one should have a clear physical model in mind or a rigorous mathematical basis. — Enrico Fermi
  20. Let the formula guide your thinking. — Someone
  21. A mathematician is a device for turning coffee into theorems. — Alfréd Rényi
  22. As long as there’s a life, there’s hope. — Stephen Hawking
  23. Every problem was once “unsolvable” until it was solved.
  24. Let us assume we know nothing, which is a reasonable approximation. — D. Kazhdan
  25. Failure is not an option. Failure is mandatory. The option is whether or not you let failure be the last thing you do. — Howard Tayler
  26. Mathematics is not about numbers, equations, computations, or algorithms: it is about understanding. — William Paul Thurston
  27. What is mathematics? It is only a systematic effort of solving puzzles posed by nature. — Shakuntala Devi
  28. Doing mathematics should always mean finding patterns and crafting beautiful and meaningful explanations. — Paul Lockhart
  29. Obvious is the most dangerous word in mathematics. — Eric Temple Bell
  30. Mathematics is the poetry of logical ideas. — Albert Einstein
  31. Only one thing can comfort your feelings like music, please your heart like paintings, stir your soul like poetry, enhance your wisdom like philosophy, and improve your life like technology — Mathematics. — Klein
